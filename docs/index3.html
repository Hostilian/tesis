Chapter 1: Introduction

1.1 Background & Motivation

In the modern digital economy, information engineering has expanded beyond terrestrial borders. The rise of Earth Observation (EO) satellite constellations, coupled with cloud-based geospatial computing platforms, has birthed a new domain: Space-Based Economic Intelligence.

Satellite remote sensing offers a continuous, independent, and spatially explicit alternative. Civilian constellations funded by the European Space Agency (ESA Copernicus) and the United States Geological Survey (USGS/NASA) capture high-resolution multi-spectral, radar, and atmospheric data daily.

1.2 Research Questions

  • RQ1: How reliably can open satellite spectral APIs identify localized industrial and mining resource anomalies compared to ground truth records?
  • RQ2: What is the correlation between VIIRS night-time light fluctuations and macroeconomic indicators in the target regions?
  • RQ3: What pipeline latency and processing constraints limit the scalability of free satellite APIs?
